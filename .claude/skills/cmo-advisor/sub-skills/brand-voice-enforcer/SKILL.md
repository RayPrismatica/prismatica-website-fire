## Writing Rules

### Rule 1: Use Active Voice
- âœ… "We analyze competitive dynamics"
- âŒ "Competitive dynamics are analyzed"

### Rule 2: Prefer Short Sentences
- Average sentence length: 15-20 words
- Vary for rhythm, but default to concise

### Rule 3: Front-Load Important Information
- Main point in first sentence
- Supporting detail follows
- Inverted pyramid structure

### Rule 4: Use Concrete Nouns and Strong Verbs
- âœ… "Build intelligence products"
- âŒ "Facilitate the development of strategic assets"

### Rule 5: Cut Adverbs and Qualifiers
- âœ… "This works"
- âŒ "This generally tends to work quite well in most cases"

### Rule 6: Avoid Buzzwords
**Banned words/phrases:**
- Synergy, synergize
- Paradigm shift
- Game-changer
- Revolutionary
- Cutting-edge (use "advanced" if needed)
- Innovative (show, don't tell)
- World-class
- Best-in-class
- Leverage (use "use")
- Utilize (use "use")
- Solutions (be specific)
- Next-generation

### Rule 7: Quantify When Possible
- âœ… "15 Fortune 500 clients"
- âŒ "Leading companies trust us"

## Content Type Guidelines

### Website Copy
- Scannable (short paragraphs, bullet lists)
- Front-loaded (key info first)
- Benefit-focused (what user gets)
- CTA clear and direct ("Enquire â†’" not "Learn More")

### Blog Posts/Articles
- Insight-driven (teach, don't sell)
- Evidence-based (data, examples, case studies)
- Structured (clear headers, logical flow)
- Byline credibility (author expertise clear)

### Email
- Subject line: Clear and specific (no clickbait)
- First sentence: Why this matters to recipient
- Body: Brief and actionable
- CTA: Single, clear next step

### Social Media (LinkedIn Primary)
- Start with insight or question
- Provide value (framework, data, perspective)
- Minimal self-promotion
- Professional, conversational tone

### Case Studies
- Context (client challenge in 2-3 sentences)
- Approach (specific methodology)
- Outcomes (quantified results)
- Intelligence product (what was created)
- Quote (authentic testimonial if available)

## Review Process

### Step 1: Initial Read
Read content once without editing. Ask:
- What is the main point?
- Is it clear on first read?
- Does it sound like Prismatica?

### Step 2: Voice Check
Review against voice principles:
- Quiet confidence? (no boasting, let work speak)
- Zero noise? (no jargon, no fluff)
- Maximum clarity? (direct, precise)

### Step 3: Line Edit
Edit for precision:
- Cut unnecessary words
- Replace vague with specific
- Eliminate buzzwords
- Strengthen verbs
- Remove adverbs

### Step 4: Structure Check
Ensure logical flow:
- Front-loaded (important info first)
- Clear hierarchy (headers, lists)
- Scannable (short paragraphs)
- Transitions smooth

### Step 5: Final Polish
Check details:
- Grammar and punctuation
- Consistent terminology
- Accurate claims
- Appropriate tone for context

## Red Flags to Watch For

When reviewing content, these are automatic rejections:

1. **Empty Superlatives**: "Best," "leading," "world-class" without evidence
2. **Buzzword Density**: More than one buzzword per paragraph
3. **Emotional Manipulation**: "Imagine," "feel," "picture this"
4. **Corporate Speak**: "Synergize," "leverage," "paradigm"
5. **Vague Promises**: "Better results," "improved outcomes" (quantify!)
6. **Long-Winded Openings**: More than 2 sentences before main point
7. **Visual Violations**: Colors, fonts, spacing not per VISUAL_IDENTITY.md
